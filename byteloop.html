The Problem

For Users — money disappears

A 2GB/day plan, 1.2GB used by midnight — 0.8GB disappears forever. You paid for it, but couldn't use it in time.

There's no warning, no countdown, no way to know how much is about to expire until it's already gone.

Across 600 million+ subscribers, this waste compounds into thousands of crores returning zero value every month.

Telecom companies lose nothing when your data expires. You do — and there's currently no way to claim it back.

For The Industry — no incentive

Carriers have no commercial reason to fix expiry — unused data is effectively free revenue from their side.

No major app currently tracks daily usage against a live countdown, so the problem stays invisible to users.

Building this without carrier buy-in means waiting for permission that may never come — unless it works at the OS level instead.

BytesLoop is designed to launch without operator partnership, proving demand first and negotiating from traction, not hope.

How BytesLoop Works

1
Track
Read usage at the OS level

BytesLoop reads daily data usage via the device's native network stats API (Android TrafficStats / iOS NEFlowMetaData) — no SIM access or carrier agreement required.

2
Predict
Forecast tonight's expiry

An on-device model projects how much data will go unused before midnight based on your remaining hours and usage pattern — flagged inside a 2-hour pre-reset window.

3
Convert
Turn waste into credit

Once the reset confirms data actually expired, it's logged and converted into BytesLoop credits at a transparent, fixed rate — no speculative crediting.

4
Spend
Redeem whenever you want

Credits accumulate in your wallet permanently. Redeem for plan discounts, partner services, or save them indefinitely — your money doesn't vanish, so neither should your value.
